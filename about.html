About the Critics Company

the critics company is a filmmaking collective based in Kaduna, Nigeria popularly known for making sci-fi films with mobile phones, since being founded in 2015, we have been driven by the desire to create magical experiences through film for ourselves and our community as a whole, this is the core of every production translating time, experiences, thoughts and feelings using art as a medium.

our work has been able to cross borders through different means, ranging from features on internationally recognized platforms like BBC, Reuters, Al-Jazeera, Indie Film Festival, The art of protest by gestalten, to collaborations with top brands and great minds in the likes of Kaspersky, Jacobjonasthecompany, Samsung, Dan Mace to acquiring titles and awards such as the first webby nomination from Nigeria, Our short films Z: The Beginning and Timothee winning best short film and best international short film at the African Smartphone International Film festival and Indie Film Fest respectively, After recently being selected as a part of the YouTube Black voices class of 22, we are understanding that the secret to success is in every drop of perseverance you inhabit.

we are constantly pushing the boundaries of what you can achieve when you put your mind to it, whether you have a professional camera, a BIG film crew, a smart phone, DIY equipment and a group of like minded individuals.

Members of the collective

Richard Yusuff is a Kaduna based Filmmaker, Writer, Script Supervisor, Photographer, Vfx Artist, Editor and member of the filmmaking Collective - The Critics. He has starred as an actor in over 10 shortfilms produced by the collective. He’s a content creation enthusiast and is a Youtube content curator. He has doubled with both cast and crew roles in various productions. He worked as a Vfx artist in the Nigerian Netflix Blockbuster “King of Boys - The Return of the King” and holds a pretty decent filmmaking portfolio. He spoke and represented Nigeria in the African Teens Summit amidst other young vibrant African youths like King Nahh, Dj Swith Ghana, Ellie Scotte and Megan Werner. He also made to the YouTube black voices creators class of 2022.

Raymond J. Yusuff is a Kaduna Based Filmmaker, VFX and CG generalist, Director, writer, cinematographer and member of the filmmaking collective - The Critics. He’s a diligent film pioneer who’s work has been showcased across the globe. He has played very key roles in the actualization of well commended works in the film scene. Judging from his work on Juju Stories by CJ ‘Fiery’ Obasi, Ogunola by The Critics, King of Boys: The Return of the King and various other films.

Ronald Yusuff is a Kaduna Based Filmmaker, Camera Operator, Model, Producer and member of the filmmaking Collective - The Critics. He stands out as an eminently straight forward film enthusiast who’s approach serves as a balance to film processes in the company. He has manned the camera for numerous productions in the Company and has also doubled as an Editor and Vfx artist in other film Endeavours. You just know he’s made for the lens with his alluring facial features which has led to him starring in films made by the collective and gaining some recognition as a Model. He spoke in the 2019 Dare conference and has graced the stages of other various functions. You can reach him on these social media handles.

RVictor Josiah is a Kaduna Based Filmmaker, graphics artist, Vfx artist, Editor, Guitarist and member of the Filmmaking Collective - The Critics. He’s a versatile thinker who formulates Ideas from a very broad perspective. His proficiency in motion graphics makes him a very competent Vfx artist and that shows in work on King of Boys: The return of the King. He spoke at the 2019 Kaduna Book and Arts Festival (KABAFEST) and has showcased impeccable knowledge on various works by the collective. He can be reached on these handles.

Godwin Josiah is a Kaduna Based Filmmaker, Musical Artiste, Director and member of the Filmmaking collective - The Critics. He has spear headed multiple film endeavours by the collective and his artistic vision spreads across various aspects of filmic inventiveness. His musical project “Road to Nirvana” just showcases his ability to bring stories to life even from a musical standpoint. His method of filmmaking implores the audience to being in sync with the story thereby helping them gravitate to wherever it leads. He can be reached on these social media handles.
